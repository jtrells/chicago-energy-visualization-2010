About

When using this project, please cite:
J. Trelles, D. Lee, S. Derrible, G.E. Marai, "Visual Analysis of a Smart City's Energy Consumption", Multimodal Technologies and Interaction Special Issue on Interactive Visualizations for Sustainability, pp.1-13, May 2019.

The Chicago Energy Visualization project allows the exploration of gas and electricity consumption data in the city of Chicago. This open-source project was designed and implemented in 2017 by researchers at the Electronic Visualization Laboratory (EVL) at the University of Illinois at Chicago, under the direction of Prof. G.Elisabeta Marai. Team members include Juan Trelles Trabucco (EVL) and Complex and Sustainable Urban Networks Laboratory members Dongwoo Lee and Prof. Sybil Derrible.

This project integrates City of Chicago 2010 data (Energy 2010, Boundaries - Community Areas, Boundaries - Census Tracts 2010, Boundaries, Census Blocks 2010 Inventory) that has been modified from its original source, https://data.cityofchicago.org/, the official website of the Chicago Data Portal. The City of Chicago makes no claims as to the content, accuracy, timeliness, or completeness of any of the data provided at this site. The data provided at this site is subject to change at any time. It is understood that the data provided at this site is being used at one’s own risk.
